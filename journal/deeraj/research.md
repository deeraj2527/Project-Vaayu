## Session: 2026-05-02 — Vaayu Detailed BOM & Lifecycle Cost Build-up — Engineering-Grade Estimate

### Summary
Title: Vaayu Detailed BOM & Lifecycle Cost Build-up — Engineering-Grade Estimate
Type: Research / Work Done
Summary: Built a system-by-system Bill of Materials for the Vaayu Track 1 prototype (CH750-derived all-metal airframe, Rotax 912 ULS, Garmin G3X experimental suite, 600 kg MTOW, 2-seat). Synthesized pricing from public kit-aircraft analogs (Van's RV-12iS, Zenith CH750, Sonex), Aircraft Spruce 2024–25 catalog levels, and Indian fabrication shop rates. Built a complete lifecycle cost model from concept through first year of demo operations. Identified 16+ hidden costs commonly missed by first-time builders. Mapped India sourcing gaps and certification bottlenecks at component level. Challenged 8 assumptions in the existing concept report with quantified counter-positions.
BOM Roll-up (planning baseline ₹84/USD):

Section A Airframe Structure: Low ₹4.25L / Realistic ₹6.78L / High ₹10.11L. Includes 4130 CrMo tube (~40-50kg), 6061-T6 sheet (~80kg), 2024-T3 sheet (~50kg, import-dependent), 7075-T6 spar caps (~10kg, mill-cert mandatory for cert path), AN bolts/nuts/washers (~1100 pcs total assorted), 3500 solid rivets (Triton/SPS India), 800 blind rivets (Bulten India).
Section B Cockpit: Low ₹1.47L / Realistic ₹2.64L / High ₹4.65L. Two seats with composite shells, 4-point harnesses (TSO 2-3x experimental), acrylic canopy bubble (~1.2m × 0.9m, local Pune/Bangalore fab), 4130 canopy frame, control sticks, rudder pedal weldments, instrument panel structure (CNC waterjet locally).
Section C Flight Controls: Low ₹0.81L / Realistic ₹1.49L / High ₹2.78L. 4130/aluminum pushrods (~12m), AN rod ends (~30 pcs imported), CNC bellcranks, SS control cables (1/16" 7×7, ~30m), Nicopress sleeves, manual or electric trim and flap actuation.
Section D Propulsion Integration: Low ₹1.65L / Realistic ₹2.98L / High ₹5.19L. 4130 engine mount (Rotax-specific truss, local fab from CH750 drawings), 2-piece composite cowling (fibreglass or CF), Lord/Barry vibration isolators (4x imported), spinner, aluminum cooling baffles, exhaust system (mostly imported from Vetterman/Aircraft Exhaust), oil cooler, coolant radiator, push-pull control cables.
Section E Fuel System: Low ₹1.16L / Realistic ₹2.04L / High ₹3.46L. Two ~50L wing tanks (6061 or 5052 welded with Pro-Seal), Andair 3-way AN6 fuel selector (imported, no Indian equivalent), gascolator (imported), Facet/Walbro 4-7 PSI 12V boost pump (auto-grade substitute possible), Aeroquip/PTFE AN6 fuel lines (~10m, mostly imported, Parker India for some grades), capacitive fuel quantity senders (G3X-compatible).
Section F Brakes/Wheels: Low ₹1.03L / Realistic ₹1.78L / High ₹2.99L. Matco MC-4 master cylinders (2x toe brake, imported), Matco single-puck calipers (2x imported), machinable discs (Indian shop possible), Parker India SS braid PTFE brake lines, 5.00x5 or 6.00x6 wheels with tubes (3x, Cheng Shin/Aero Classic via domestic dealers), MIL-H-5606 brake fluid.
Section G Electrical: Low ₹1.31L / Realistic ₹2.32L / High ₹3.97L. 12V 18Ah AGM battery (Concorde RG-25XC equiv. or Amaron/Exide industrial AGM for low end), 200A continuous battery contactor, 200A intermittent starter contactor, ~18 W58-series circuit breakers (5-30A, imported, Indian gap), ~150m MIL-W-22759 aircraft wire (Apar Industries domestic possible pending spec verification), MIL-DTL-38999 connectors (imported, Amphenol India limited SKUs), ~80 hrs harness assembly labour.
Section H Avionics (Garmin G3X experimental): Low ₹10.11L / Realistic ₹12.21L / High ₹14.87L. GDU 460 10.6" PFD/MFD (~$2300 list, ₹1.80-2.30L), GSU 25D ADAHRS (~$900 list), GMU 22 magnetometer, GTP 59 OAT probe, GAP 26 heated pitot-static probe (heated preferred for India IFR ambition), GPS 20A WAAS, GEA 24 engine interface with EGT/CHT/OP/OT/fuel-flow sensor pack, GFC 500 autopilot with 2 servos and GMC control head (₹2.10L realistic, major cost driver), GTR 200 VHF com, GTX 345R Mode S 1090 ES out transponder, GMA 245 Bluetooth audio panel, G5 backup AHRS (DGCA dual-display expectation), 4× antennas, install kit and connectors.
Section I Control Surface Electronics: Low ₹0.29L / Realistic ₹0.48L / High ₹0.76L. Linear pot flap position sensor, rotary pot trim sensor, Floscan/EI fuel flow transducer (imported, GEA 24-compatible), manifold pressure sender.
Section J Safety (excl. BRS): Low ₹0.92L / Realistic ₹1.39L / High ₹2.11L. ACK E-04 or Kannad Integra 406 MHz ELT (imported, no Indian equivalent, DGCA-mandated), ELT antenna and mount, 2.5lb Halotron cockpit fire extinguisher, aviation first aid kit, basic CO/smoke detector. Optional BRS Ballistic Recovery System (airframe parachute) ₹4.50-8.50L additional, recommended for STOL operating in Himalayas.
Section K Finishes & Consumables: Low ₹1.17L / Realistic ₹2.04L / High ₹3.64L. Henkel India Alodine 1200S, AkzoNobel India 2K MIL-PRF-23377 epoxy primer (~10L), polyurethane topcoat (~6L), 80-120 hrs paint application labour (often farmed to auto refinish shop), PR-1422 Pro-Seal fuel tank sealant (2kg, imported, PPG India limited, 6-month shelf life issue), Huntsman India Araldite 2011/2015 structural epoxy, silicone RTV, Loctite 243/271, interior carpet/panels/finishing, registration markings/placards per CAR §14.2.
Section L Powerplant: Low ₹13.26L / Realistic ₹16.59L / High ₹19.77L. Rotax 912 ULS new complete with airbox and ignition modules (₹13.50L realistic, via Jomo Aviation or direct import, landed cost includes ~18% duties), initial oil and coolant fill per Rotax SI, basic Rotax service tools kit (one-time), Sensenich/GSC 72" 2-blade fixed-pitch composite propeller (₹2.40L realistic, ground-adjustable; constant-speed adds ₹3-5L), Rotax-specific prop hub and bolts.
Landing Gear (called out separately): Low ₹0.89L / Realistic ₹1.57L / High ₹2.66L. 5160 spring steel main gear leaf springs (2x, machined and heat-treated, local spring shop with heat treat), machined steel nose gear leg and fork, oleo or bungee nose gear shock, optional composite wheel pants (3x, often deferred).

Total BOM excluding BRS: Low ₹38.32L / Realistic ₹54.31L / High ₹76.96L. Total BOM with BRS: Low ₹42.82L / Realistic ₹60.81L / High ₹85.46L. USD equivalent of realistic ≈ $64,700, broadly consistent with built RV-12iS in 2024 USD ($80-110k), slightly lower because Indian local fabrication of airframe avoids Van's quickbuild kit margin.
Cost driver concentration: Powerplant 31.1% of realistic BOM, Avionics 22.9%, Airframe structure 12.7%. These three together = 66.7% of total — primary optimization targets.
Reality check vs concept report: Existing report estimated ₹30-41L for IC variant. My realistic build-up at ₹54L is 35% higher. Deltas: avionics fully itemised including install hardware (+₹2L), ELT/brake hardware/fuel system fittings broken out (+₹3L), realistic paint and finish labour (+₹2L), engine landed at ₹13.5L not ₹12L (customs + freight). Recommendation: use ₹54L as planning baseline, ₹77L as funded contingency.
Lifecycle Phase Cost Breakdown:

Phase 1 Concept & Design (Months 0-6): Low ₹8.2L / Realistic ₹23.5L / High ₹70.0L. CAD software (Solidworks Standard ~₹4L/seat/yr or Fusion 360 ~₹50k/yr), FEA/CFD tools (free at concept stage like ANSYS Student/SimScale, commercial for cert), aero analysis (XFLR5 free, ANSYS Fluent paid), SysML/requirements tooling (Cameo or IBM DOORS, DOORS expensive and can defer), 2 engineers × 6 months at blended ₹1L/mo (founder equity vs paid), external structural consultant for stress analysis sign-off, aviation IP attorney for Zenith licence negotiation (hidden cost), DGCA pre-application travel and prep, optional NAL Bangalore wind tunnel/sub-scale testing.
Phase 2 Prototype Build (Months 6-24): Low ₹58.4L / Realistic ₹103.1L / High ₹171.0L. Full BOM ₹38-77L, 2 builders × 18 months labour (founder vs hired AME assistant), AME consultant for oversight and DGCA inspection sign-offs (critical), hangar/workshop rent 18 months at ₹15-60k/mo, tooling (pneumatic rivet guns, bucking bars, drill bits, clecos — clecos alone ₹40-60k for 3000+ pcs), build jigs and fixtures (fuselage cradle, wing jig, reusable), 250A TIG inverter welder if not outsourced, compressor and paint booth time (often rented), shipping and customs brokerage for engine/avionics/BRS (often forgotten ₹3-6.5L), 10-15% material wastage allowance.
Phase 3 Testing (Months 24-30): Low ₹10.5L / Realistic ₹25.3L / High ₹53.5L. Test instrumentation beyond G3X (data logger, vibration, strain gauges if structural data needed), pitot-static cal rig and leak tester, fuel quantity calibration setup, ground vibration test setup for modal analysis if cert-intent, 40-hr flight test programme fuel/oil/consumables at ~₹6k/hr operating cost, test pilot fees (PPL open-rating experienced, Indian test pilots scarce, premium pricing), DGCA inspection fees and travel for multiple inspections (pre-cover, final, flight test), aviation hull and 3rd party insurance for test flights (India limited, expect 5-8% of hull value), WPC/Aero-mobile licence application and legal, test airfield slot fees if using flying club facility, scheduled maintenance during 40-hr programme, contingency for test-induced damage and squawks (every test programme has issues).
Phase 4 Iteration & Refinement (Months 18-36 overlapping): Low ₹2.3L / Realistic ₹9.0L / High ₹26.5L. Cowling redesign and refab (typical, first cowling rarely fits), control system refinement (linkage geometry tweaks), avionics reconfiguration (panel layout iteration), landing gear adjustment or re-spring if trail/stance wrong, aerodynamic fairings and seal kits added post-test, structural mods if test reveals weak points (hopefully zero), software updates for G3X config and ArduPilot tuning (mostly time not parts).
Phase 5 Pre-Production Setup (Months 30-48): Low ₹40.0L / Realistic ₹97.0L / High ₹226.0L. Production tooling (master jigs, bend dies, drill templates — order of magnitude bigger than prototype jigs), QC infrastructure (calibrated mics, gauges, NDT setup), documentation system (PLM, drawing management, critical for kit replication), builder manual development with photo + text (often under-resourced), vendor agreements and first-article inspections (per-supplier sign-off), initial 5-kit-lot component inventory (bulk discount but inventory cost), ERP/order management system (Zoho One scaling to NetSuite), facility expansion small assembly hangar (if owning vs leasing), staff hire 3-5 production techs with ramp-up training (first-year fully-loaded).
Phase 6 Deployment Year 1 (Months 30-60+ ongoing): Low ₹11.8L / Realistic ₹31.8L / High ₹79.0L. Demo tour ops (fuel, oil, ground transport annual), trade show participation (Aero India, Wings India, AeroExpo Friedrichshafen — booths + travel + freight), marketing materials (video production, brochures, web — YouTube channel ops part of this), Rotax scheduled maintenance (100-hr, annual), operational insurance per year, ongoing hangar per year, customer demo flights pilot fees and fuel.

Lifecycle Total Concept → First Year Demo Ops: Low ₹131L (1.31 Cr) / Realistic ₹290L (2.90 Cr) / High ₹626L (6.26 Cr). Plan around ₹3 Cr to get from concept to first demo ops with one prototype plus production-ready setup. Add ₹17-34 Cr more for the parallel cert program per previous session discussion.
Hidden Costs Surfaced (16 items typically missed):
Cleco fasteners ₹40-60k for 3000+ pcs. Drill bit consumption ₹15-25k (each #30/#40 bit dies fast in 4130). Material wastage 10-15% = ₹3-6L (first-time builders cut wrong repeatedly). Shipping and customs brokerage ₹3-6L (FOB engine becomes 18% more landed). AME consulting hours ₹3-5L (free favour from a friend usually isn't). Insurance during build ₹2-4L over 18 months (hull-not-in-motion + premises liability). Hangar/workshop rent ₹3-8L over 18 months (founder garage rarely works for 600kg AC). Test pilot retainer ₹3-5L (few qualified Indian test pilots, command premium). WPC + MHA application legal ₹1-2L (needs lawyer who knows aerospace). BRS parachute repack every 6 yrs ₹1-2L (recurring opex if installed). Engine break-in oil and filter changes ₹40-60k first 25 hr (Rotax SI requires specific oils and intervals). Avionics database subscriptions ₹50k-1L/yr (Garmin Pilot, navigation databases). Documentation translation/formatting for DGCA ₹2-4L (English, AED standards). Failed-test redo of structural article #2 ₹5-15L (cert-program risk if first article fails proof load). Annual airworthiness inspection post-CoA ₹50k-1L/yr. Founder opportunity cost / unpaid labour ₹15-30L over 24 months (real cost, equity-funded but cash-flow real). Warranty/support reserves for kit buyers 5-8% of kit revenue.
India Sourcing Gaps (12 items):
AN bolts/nuts full range (mostly imported via Aircraft Spruce/SkyGeek, long lead, stock 2 kits worth at first import). Cleco fasteners (no Indian maker, bulk import reusable ₹40-60k one-time). Avionics MIL-DTL-38999 connectors (Amphenol India limited SKUs, import for first 5 builds, volume contract later). W58-series circuit breakers (no Indian maker, Aircraft Spruce import, Carlingswitch India for limited SKUs). Matco brake calipers and master cylinders (no Indian maker, import, Indian machine shop can replicate calipers but not certify). Andair fuel selector valve, gascolator, AN fittings (no aviation-grade Indian source, Andair/Parker imported). 406 MHz ELT (no Indian maker, DGCA-mandated, ACK/Kannad import, lobby for COTS acceptance). Pro-Seal PR-1422 fuel tank sealant (PPG India limited, import, 6-month shelf life makes this tricky). Aviation tyres 5.00x5/6.00x6 (Cheng Shin/Aero Classic available via dealers, domestic dealer network okay). Composite prepreg T700/T800 (Tata Advanced Materials but limited grades, import for prototype, volume commitment unlocks domestic). BRS parachute (no Indian maker, import, single biggest gap if BRS chosen). Aviation-grade MIL-W-22759 wire (Apar Industries spec verification needed, send sample for spec audit otherwise import).
Certification Bottlenecks Identified (items that work for prototype but block TC later):
Rotax 912 ULS non-cert install — prototype OK, TC blocker, need 912 S2 with cert install per Rotax SI, plan to swap to 912 S2 for cert article. Garmin G3X experimental — TC blocker, need G3X Certified or G500 TXi, same display geometry but different SKU and ~3x price. ArduPilot autopilot — no DAL evidence, use GFC 500 in cert config. AI/ML advisory layer — no precedent for ML primary functions, position as DAL E advisory only. Indian-fabricated 4130 tube without mill certs — material traceability fails for cert, source mill-cert tube from Bohler/imported for cert article. Local sheet-metal fabrication — process control gaps, need DOA-controlled production for cert. LFP battery pack for electric variant — no DGCA precedent for aviation Li-ion, multi-year cert pathway development needed. Custom Indian-made BMS — DAL B/C software not demonstrated, either commission cert-grade BMS or partner with cert-experienced vendor. Local upholstery/interior — flammability per CS-23 §853, use cert-rated fabrics for cert article. Acrylic canopy from local fabricator — bird-strike/impact testing needed, qualified canopy manufacturer for cert. Pitot-static system without TSO instruments — TSO'd instruments needed for cert, G3X Certified path includes TSO ADAHRS.
Critical Assumptions Challenged (8 with quantified counter-positions):
(1) "₹30-41L kit cost IC variant" — materials + headline imports only, realistic all-in build is ₹54L BOM plus ₹40-50L Phase 2 surrounding costs (labour, hangar, tooling, shipping, AME), first prototype runs ~₹100L cash not ₹40L. (2) "Garmin G3X bridges to electric variant" — partially true, GEA 24 reconfigurable for custom CAN parameters but cert-grade integration with HV powertrain has zero precedent, plan parallel custom EMS for electric track. (3) "Indian fabrication is cheaper" — true at unit cost, NOT true at total cost when factoring in first-article wastage, inter-city transport (Rajkot sheet shop, Pune welding), AME oversight visits — complete imported quickbuild kit (Van's RV-12iS at ~₹50L landed) may be cheaper than locally fabricated at unit volume of 1. (4) "Open-source avionics saves ₹7-9L" — true for hardware cost, but engineering effort to integrate, debug, certify exceeds savings; open-source path makes sense for production kit (after engineering amortised) not prototype. (5) "Crowdfunding will fund prototype" — ₹40-60L target realistic for awareness not full build, most successful aviation crowdfunds raise 30-50% of build cost, plan crowdfunding as market-validation tool not primary funding. (6) "Phase 2 (electric) and Phase 3 (hybrid) follow Phase 1" — implicitly assumes Phase 1 succeeds, realistic outcome is Phase 1 takes 30 months instead of 18 and consumes Phase 2 budget, electric variant slips by 18+ months, build Phase 1 timeline+budget to absorb this. (7) "Battery cert pathway is a Phase 2 problem" — DGCA has no framework for aviation Li-ion, start regulatory engagement on this in parallel with Phase 1 not after, otherwise flying electric prototype with no airworthiness path. (8) "YouTube channel funds itself via AdSense" — at 10k subs target, AdSense yields ~₹15-30k/month, real value is audience for crowdfunding and kit pre-orders not ad revenue, don't model AdSense as material income.
Three Cost-Optimisation Recommendations:
(1) Defer Phase 5 production tooling until after Phase 1 prototype validates design — Phase 5 spend before validated design = sunk cost when design changes, re-sequencing cuts ~₹50-70L of risk capital. (2) Phase 1 with minimum-viable avionics — drop GFC 500 autopilot ₹2.1L and BRS ₹6.5L from prototype #1, add to prototype #2 once airframe proven, saves ₹8-9L upfront and reduces test program risk (no autopilot to debug while validating handling qualities). (3) Single-airframe dual-purpose — design Phase 1 prototype so post-demo it can be re-engined to electric (Phase 2) without scrapping, saves ₹25-30L vs building second airframe, already implied by common engine mount interface, make explicit in design freeze.
Funding Plan Recommendation:
Pre-seed ₹40-60L Months 0-6 (Phase 1 design + start of build). Seed ₹1.5-2 Cr Months 6-24 (Phase 2 prototype completion + Phase 3 testing). Series A ₹8-12 Cr Months 24-36 (Phase 5 production setup + cert program kick-off). Series B ₹20-30 Cr Months 36-60 (cert program through TC). Current concept understates Phase 1+2 by ~40% — build investor decks against realistic numbers, investors discount under-budgeted plans more harshly than honest-but-larger ones.
Decisions:

Use ₹54L as Phase 1 BOM planning baseline, ₹77L as funded contingency (rejecting concept report's ₹30-41L as understated)
Plan for ~₹100L total cash for first prototype completion (BOM + Phase 2 surrounding costs)
Plan ~₹3 Cr concept-through-first-year-demo-ops; ~₹17-34 Cr additional for parallel cert program
Defer GFC 500 autopilot and BRS to prototype #2 to de-risk prototype #1 test programme
Defer Phase 5 production tooling investment until post-Phase 1 design validation
Design prototype #1 for re-engining to electric in Phase 2 (don't scrap and rebuild)
Start DGCA Li-ion aviation cert dialogue in parallel with Phase 1 (not after)
Treat YouTube/crowdfunding as market-validation and audience-building, not primary funding
Stock 2 kits' worth of imports (AN hardware, clecos, connectors) on first import to amortise shipping
